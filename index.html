Bhavesh Shrimali

I am a Research fellow at IITB working with Prof. Oscar Lopez-Pamies. My primary research area is theoretical and computational mechanics, where I work in the broad domain of analytical and numerical homogenization. This involves studying partial differential equations (PDEs) whose coefficients oscillate at multiple (largely different) scales. A natural manifestation of such PDEs occurs in highly heterogeneous materials such as composites. Currently I am working on the homogenization of porous/perforated composites (see our recent paper below). I am also pursuing a minor in Computational Science and Engineering

Prior to this, I was part of IIT Guwaha.

Research

I'm interested in solving fundamental problems in Applied Math and Numerical Analysis. A large component of my research spans around discretizing PDEs using the Finite Element Method, and more often than not, using the FEniCS project. My recent interests have also revolved around the applications of Deep Learning to solve problems in Mechanics. This has evolved into a field of it's own called Scientific Machine Learning (or more popularly SciML). If you are interested in it, you may want to check Chris Rackauckas' awesome software suite in Julia. I also contribute to the FEniCS Q&A forum and plan to work on extending the functionality of the development version of FEniCS dolfin-X to integrate with Deep Learning Algorithms. In my not-so-distant past, I have done (and still like doing) numerical homogenization calculations in Abaqus (using UEL, UMAT, UHYPER,...), though lately my focus has moved to more expressible and open source libraries such as FEniCS, scikit-FEM and Gridap.jl.
